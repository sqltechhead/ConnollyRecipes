## Ingredients
- [ ] 1/3 batch [croissant dough](/recipes/baking/croissantdough)
- [ ] 100g raisins or sultanas
- [ ] 2 tbsp dark rum (or orange juice)
- [ ] 1 egg (for egg wash)
- [ ] 3 tbsp apricot jam (optional, for glaze)

### Crème pâtissière
- [ ] 250ml whole milk
- [ ] 1 tsp vanilla extract (or 1 vanilla pod)
- [ ] 3 egg yolks
- [ ] 50g caster sugar
- [ ] 20g plain flour
- [ ] 20g cornflour

## Method

### Make the crème pâtissière
1. Heat the milk and vanilla in a saucepan over medium heat until just steaming. Do not boil.
2. Whisk the egg yolks, sugar, flour and cornflour together in a bowl until pale and smooth.
3. Pour the hot milk over the egg mixture in a steady stream, whisking constantly.
4. Return everything to the pan and cook over medium heat, stirring continuously, until the custard thickens and bubbles for 1 minute.
5. Press a sheet of cling film directly onto the surface and refrigerate until completely cold.

### Soak the raisins
6. Soak the raisins in rum or orange juice for at least 30 minutes, then drain well.

### Assemble
7. On a lightly floured surface, roll the dough out to a 40x30cm rectangle, about 4mm thick.
8. Spread the cold crème pâtissière evenly across the dough, leaving a 2cm gap along one long edge.
9. Scatter the drained raisins evenly over the cream.
10. Roll the dough tightly from the long edge without the gap, forming a neat log.
11. Trim the uneven ends, then slice into 4 rounds about 4cm thick.

### Proving
12. Place cut-side up on a lined baking tray, spaced well apart.
13. Cover loosely and prove at room temperature for 2-3 hours until puffy.

### Baking
14. Preheat the oven to 190°C (170°C fan).
15. Brush the tops lightly with beaten egg.
16. Bake for 18-20 minutes until golden brown.
17. If glazing, heat the apricot jam with a splash of water and brush over the pastries as soon as they come out of the oven.
18. Cool on a wire rack for 10 minutes before eating.
